All The Best Artists Have A Staring Problem

What is more captivating than a face? Features vary on an individual level; each quirk distinguishes one self from another. A face constitutes the majority of the physical identity and can be considered a synecdoche of humanity.

The face exudes emotion and meaning, characterizes the things we say and expresses how we feel. Every idiosyncrasy - every freckle, wrinkle, blemish, scar- expresses the story of the face upon which it rests.

It is not difficult to understand why artists through all of time have been fixated on and fascinated by the human face.

Our second featured collection dwells on the human face as subject and object. The collection juxtaposes powerfully detailed photographs, emotive paintings, and bold graphite scrawls in a way that encapsulates the universal artistic appeal of the individual face.

We encourage you to get caught staring.
